Compositional Generalization

LLMs combine learned sub-patterns to handle situations never literally seen — genuine problem-solving, not retrieval.

~12 min
Anchor Analogy
A jazz musician who improvises by recombining learned harmonic patterns, not a jukebox that only plays stored songs.
Why This Matters
Answers "if it just guesses the next word, how does it solve novel problems?" — compositional generalization is the mechanistic answer.
After This Page, You Can Argue
After this page, you can answer the hardest skeptic question: "It solves novel problems by recombining learned patterns — the way a jazz musician improvises."
