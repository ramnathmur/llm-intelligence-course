Hallucination

LLMs sometimes generate confident, fluent, entirely false statements — a failure mode with multiple proposed causes including training data gaps, calibration failures, and decoding dynamics.

~12 min
Anchor Analogy
A brilliant consultant who, when they don't know the answer, confidently improvises something plausible rather than saying "I don't know."
Why This Matters
The strongest honest counterweight. Understanding hallucination makes the learner's intelligence claims defensible, not naive.
After This Page, You Can Argue
After this page, your intelligence claims are honest: "Yes, it's genuinely intelligent — AND it has systematic failure modes a truly understanding mind wouldn't have."
